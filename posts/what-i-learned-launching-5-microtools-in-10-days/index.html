What I Learned Launching 5 Microtools in 10 Days

Lessons from a speedrun through five micro-SaaS launches. Chaos, clarity, and a few tools that stuck.
3 min · 458 words · Matt Livingston

Let’s get this out of the way: I did not plan to build five tools in ten days.

It just happened. Like most good chaos does.

I had a backlog of ideas, an urge to ship, and a bad habit of starting projects at 11pm. What started as “let me finish one of these” turned into an accidental sprint of five microtools — each with a homepage, backend, AI integration, and a little soul stuffed in.

Here’s what I learned during that caffeine-drenched bender:

1. Nobody Cares at First — Good#

You will not go viral. You will not wake up to 10,000 users. And that’s a gift.

The early silence is freedom. It lets you suck in peace. You get to test, tweak, rebuild without a crowd breathing down your neck. Your first users will be curious ghosts. Respect them. Watch what they do. Learn from their silence.

2. One Clear Benefit > Ten Features#

The tools that resonated weren’t the ones packed with options — they were the ones with one clear, immediate payoff.

  • “Fix my copy.” (CopyKiller)
  • “Rescue my old blog post.” (Keyword Necromancer)
  • “Plan my day like a tactical strike.” (Stratify)

Each one was a single sharp edge — not a toolkit. That’s what got clicks.

3. Launching Is a Feature#

Adding a “launch moment” to your tool — even a small one — forces clarity. You write better copy. You fix the bug you’ve been ignoring. You make decisions.

Shipping fast becomes a superpower if you treat launches like iterations, not finales.

4. Timeboxes Force Ruthlessness#

I gave myself 1–2 days per tool. That means:

  • No feature creep
  • No perfectionism
  • No fragile architectures

What mattered: core value, clarity, stability. If it worked, it shipped. Done.

5. Everything You Build Is Leverage Later#

Every tool I launched this week is now:

  • A portfolio piece
  • A source of backlinks
  • A testbed for future features
  • A potential revenue stream
  • A tiny asset, running 24/7

Not all of them will “succeed.” But all of them stack.

Tools I Shipped#

If you’re curious what five tools in ten days looks like, here’s the batch:

  • 🔪 Cutlist – Ruthless action prioritizer
  • 🧠 ThinkTankz – Ask a fictional board of advisors
  • 💀 CopyKiller – Brutal AI copy editor
  • 🧟‍♂️ Keyword Necromancer – SEO resurrection tool
  • 📵 ScreenDetox – Digital eulogy + detox

Each one was a micro-SaaS — scoped tight, shipped fast.

Final Thought#

If you’re hesitating to launch: stop polishing. Pick one sharp feature. Make it solve one real pain. Then ship it.

Nobody will care — until someone does. And that’s when everything changes.
